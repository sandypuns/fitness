The Logic
Weekdays are pure lifting + 10 min incline walk at the end. Simple, time-efficient, consistent. Weekends are 55 min lifting + 35 min cardio — cardio goes after weights, not before, because glycogen is depleted post-lift which forces your body to pull more from fat stores. Push is hit twice (Mon + Sat), Pull twice (Fri + Sun), Legs once with full Friday volume. No back-to-back heavy sessions — Tue and Thu are rest days providing recovery buffers around the big compound work.

Why Cardio Goes After Lifting, Not Before
After 55 min of lifting, your muscle glycogen (carb stores) are significantly depleted. When you then do cardio, your body shifts faster toward using fat as fuel because the easy carb source is largely gone. Doing cardio first instead burns glycogen, leaves you weaker for lifting, and reduces the fat-burn shift. Lift first, always.

What Is an Incline Walk?
An incline walk is walking on the treadmill at a steep uphill angle — typically 8–12% incline at 5.5–6.5 kmph. It is not jogging. It is not a casual stroll. It is a deliberate, brisk walk uphill that keeps your heart rate in the fat-burning zone (roughly 60–70% of max HR) without the joint stress of running or the CNS fatigue of HIIT.

At 12% incline and 6 kmph you are working harder than most people jog at 0% incline. Your glutes, hamstrings, and calves are heavily recruited. Calorie burn is roughly 8–10 kcal/min — comparable to a light jog but with zero impact.

The "talk test": You should be able to speak a few words but not hold a full conversation comfortably. If you can chat easily, raise incline. If you can't speak at all, lower it slightly.

Common Mistakes on Incline Walk
  • Holding the handrails: Completely negates the incline effect. Arms should swing freely or be loose at sides.
  • Too slow at high incline: 15% incline at 3 kmph is not effective. Keep pace at 5.5–6.5 kmph.
  • Too much incline too soon: Start at 8%, build to 12% over 2 weeks. Calf strain is common if you jump straight to 15%.
  • Skipping it because you're tired: 10 min. You just did 45 min of lifting. This is non-negotiable.

Supplements — Minimal Effective Set
  • Creatine monohydrate — 5g/day, any time. Non-negotiable for recomp.
  • Whey protein — only if food protein falls below 120g. Not a magic powder.
  • Vitamin D3 + K2 — 2000 IU D3 daily. Most urban Indians are deficient.
  • Magnesium glycinate — 300mg before bed. Improves sleep quality and recovery.
  • Skip fat burners, BCAAs, pre-workout — not needed, won't move the needle at this stage.

Week 1
Foundation60–70% load
Learn movement patterns. Moderate weights — form over ego, especially squat, deadlift, bench. Don't go to failure. Log starting weights on every exercise. HIIT at 80% effort max.
Week 2
VolumeLoad ↑
Add 2.5–5 kg to compounds where Week 1 felt comfortable. Last set of each exercise: 1–2 reps from failure. Beat Week 1 sprint speeds by 0.5–1 kmph on HIIT.
Week 3
StrengthIntensity ↑
75–80% max on compounds. Add 1 working set to bench, squat, RDL. Upgrade HIIT to 10-round protocol. Incline walk bumps to 12% on weekday finishers.
Week 4
PeakMax Effort
Heaviest week. Leave 1 rep in tank on all sets. At month end: measure waist, chest, upper arms. Log all compound lift maxes for Month 2 baseline.
Realistic Timeline from 25% BF — No BS
With the updated 5-day plan you're burning more per week. Realistic fat loss: 0.5–0.75 kg/week. Still need to lose 7–9 kg of fat to see abs. That math hasn't changed.
  • Month 1 (25% → 22–23% BF): Face and arms visibly leaner. Waist down 2–4 cm. Shoulders more defined. Scale drops 1.5–2.5 kg. No abs — belly fat is last.
  • Month 2 (22% → 19–20% BF): Chest and back definition appearing. Clothes fit differently. Strength on compounds up 15–25%. Still no visible abs.
  • Month 3–4 (19% → 15–16% BF): Upper abs in good lighting. V-shape emerging. Arms and shoulders look genuinely athletic. First real before/after moment.
  • Month 5–6 (15% → 12–13% BF): Clear abs in normal lighting. Veins on forearms. Legitimately lean. Most people's definition of "ripped."
  • Month 7–9 (sub-12% BF): Stage-lean. Lower abs visible. Diet needs to be dialled in tightly.
  • Track waist circumference weekly. Scale lies — belly fat goes last. Waist shrink is the real signal.
